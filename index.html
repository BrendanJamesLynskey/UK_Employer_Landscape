    Yorkshire & Greater Manchester — the local picture

    The single most under-rated finding from this research: the Yorkshire / Lancashire / Greater Manchester arc contains an unusually rich set of employers for a hybrid HW + DSP + embedded-Linux + AI profile, even though the absolute headcount is below the Cambridge / Bristol clusters.     Methodology & caveats

    Source pipeline: per-category research used WebSearch + WebFetch against company careers pages, LinkedIn Jobs (UK filter), Indeed UK, CW Jobs, semiconductor-jobs.co.uk, UKESF directory, plus 2024–26 industry reporting (Tech Nation, Dealroom, Beauhurst, eeNews Europe, EE Times Europe). Each per-category writeup includes its own Sources section with URLs.

    Heatmap scoring: regional concentration scores are subjective 0–10 ratings combining (a) number of distinct employers in the area, (b) typical role volume in April 2026, and (c) seniority/match to the profile. Scores are not employer counts — a city with three highly-relevant Staff-level employers may score higher than one with ten junior roles.

    Posting volumes are order-of-magnitude estimates and fluctuate weekly. Many roles cross-syndicate between job boards, inflating naive totals.

    Clearance: defence employers (BAE / Leonardo / MBDA / Thales / Roke / QinetiQ / Cobham Ultra / AWE) almost always require SC or DV clearance and UK nationality / 5–10 yr UK residency. Clearance processing is 6–12 weeks.

    What's deliberately excluded: generic Big-4 consultancy "AI" roles without engineering depth; recruiter agencies; companies with no UK engineering presence — though such companies that hire UK-remote (Tenstorrent, Cerebras, Groq, etc.) appear in Category 07: Remote & International instead.

    Corrections from earlier drafts

    Two factual errors from the initial research were corrected in revision 2 (May 2026):

    • Saltaire site: the actual occupant at Salts Mill (the old Pace plc site) is CommScope — Pace was acquired by Arris in 2016, then by CommScope in 2019. Earlier drafts wrongly attributed the site to Synamedia (a separate company at Staines-upon-Thames, formed from Cisco's Video Software Solutions, sold to Permira in 2018) or to Reach plc (the unrelated newspaper publisher of the Daily Mirror, Telegraph & Argus, etc).
    • Imagination Technologies has no Leeds office. Their UK presence is Kings Langley HQ + Cambridge.

    Calrec Audio (Hebden Bridge) — pro-broadcast audio mixing consoles — was added in revision 2 as an obvious and direct fit that the initial research missed.
