# Category 09 — Motorsport · High-Performance Powertrain · Auto / EV Drives

> FPGA motorsport ECUs · KERS/MGU control · 800 V SiC traction inverters · CAN/FlexRay/AutomotiveEthernet · ASIL-D · Real-time embedded C/C++ · MATLAB/Simulink · multiphase DCDC for traction & aux rails · battery management · axial-flux machines

## Summary

The UK is the world capital of motorsport engineering — "Motorsport Valley" runs roughly **Brackley → Silverstone → Northampton → Milton Keynes → Brixworth → Bicester → Woking → Grove**, and is unusually open to staff-level multidisciplinary engineers with HW + FPGA + DSP + power-electronics backgrounds. The crossover with **EV / HPP** (high-performance powertrain) work is now total: every F1 PU team, every hypercar OEM, and every motorsport-spinout consultancy is hiring traction-inverter, BMS, motor-control, and ECU engineers, increasingly with on-FPGA real-time control loops at 100 kHz+.

For a Leeds-based engineer, the realistic in-region options are **Bentley (Crewe, ~80 mi)** and **JLR (Manchester SDV / Gaydon, ~1 h)**. Everything below Birmingham is a relocation or a hybrid 2-3 days/week pattern. Several roles are remote-friendly for senior IC work (signal-processing, FPGA RTL, controls modelling). Live April-2026 listings: **~150–250 UK postings** matching motorsport-ECU / traction-inverter / motor-control / BMS keywords across LinkedIn, Indeed, and motorsportjobs.com. McLaren Applied alone has had **FPGA Designer + Principal Embedded SW** posted continuously through Q1 2026.

## Yorkshire focus — the harder honest answer

**There is essentially no motorsport HPP cluster in Yorkshire.** The realistic options for an in-region motorsport role are:

1. **JLR Manchester SDV hub (~1 h M62)** — closest match for SDV / EV / ADAS embedded Linux, but more "automotive software" than classical motorsport HPP.
2. **Bentley Crewe (~1 h 30 min)** — production-OEM EV programme, multidisciplinary HW + power-electronics + Linux IVI work.
3. **Remote-friendly seniors at McLaren Applied / Cosworth Electronics / WAE / DSD** — all four periodically post hybrid 2 days/week roles; the FPGA / RTL / controls work is well suited to remote IC patterns.
4. **ITM Power Sheffield** (kV-scale multiphase DCDC for hydrogen electrolysers — *adjacent* to motorsport HPP power electronics but a different application).

If you are willing to relocate or run a 3-day-per-week southern pattern, **McLaren Applied (Woking)** is the closest 1:1 fit on the entire UK map for an HW + FPGA + embedded + multiphase-power profile, with Mercedes HPP Brixworth and Cosworth Electronics Cambridge close behind.

## Notes & caveats

- **Hours and intensity** — F1 teams (Mercedes HPP, Red Bull, Aston Martin, Williams) are intense around the season calendar; weekend/anti-social hours are the norm during race weeks. McLaren Applied, WAE, Cosworth Electronics, Ricardo, YASA, and Drive System Design are largely conventional engineering hours.
- **Cost-cap-driven hiring (F1)** — recruitment cycles follow the FIA cost cap; hiring waves come every 12–18 months.
- **Most exciting 2025–28 trend** — 2026 F1 power-unit regulation change (50% electrical, sustainable fuels) has triggered a multi-year hiring boom across HPP / Red Bull / Aston / Williams; this is the strongest motorsport hiring market in a decade.
- **Hypercars (Murray T.33, Aston Valhalla, Lotus Evija, McLaren W1)** are pulling motorsport-grade electronics into road-car volumes for the first time; the entire ecosystem (YASA, Cosworth, McLaren Applied, Ricardo) is benefitting.
- **Defence crossover** — WAE, Ricardo, Prodrive all have growing defence EV / hybrid programmes (battlefield power packs, hybrid armoured vehicles); SC clearance becomes useful.
- **Why this category exists separately** — motorsport HPP roles overlap categories 02 (silicon adjacent), 03 (FPGA), 04 (PI/SI/power), 06 (embedded Linux), but the recruiting market is its own ecosystem with its own job boards (motorsportjobs.com) and its own cultural norms.
